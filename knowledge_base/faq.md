## EVENT CHARGES & OVERAGES

### How do event charges work?

Cutco pays for our events ahead of time, communicating with Alan as well as the promoter of the
event, based on the price they are charging us. These charges are then transferred after the event
to whoever the rep was that worked that event.

- You may have your charges applied all at once one of the weeks after the event, or in up to 6-7
  installments. The default is installments.
- You do NOT have to pay anything out of pocket. It is handled in the back end.
- Charges sent in to be applied to reps will not be adjusted unless it is coordinator error. Even
  then, sometimes Olean will not accept adjustments — especially after the 1st charge.

### What are overages?

Overages are a small, capped fee (maximum of 3 shifts per event) — currently $22 per shift — that
help cover essential operational costs of running the Events Team across all shows and marketplaces.

Overage funds go toward:
- Replacing damaged or aging booths (especially at venues like Mesa Marketplace)
- Backdrops, table gear, signage, and materials
- Storm damage repairs (common in outdoor events)
- Shared team supplies (order forms, service request forms, deal pads)
- Occasional team meals and support during long events
- Preseason equipment prep and storage unit costs

Every rep — regardless of role or seniority — contributes. Coordinators and top reps also pay
overages. Participation in the Events Team is optional, but being part of a multi-million dollar
program comes with shared responsibilities.

## TEAM TIERS & ROAD MAP

### What are the team tiers?

RSD has four tiers: Freshman, JV (Junior Varsity), Varsity, and Elite. Your tier determines which
events you can pick, your shift picking order, and your responsibilities.

## SHIFT PICKING RULES

### How does shift picking work?

1. Must have your Shift Pick List (event name and days) before the day of the meeting
2. Your TIER is determined by your Key or Traditional shift average from the previous 12 months (minimum 10 shifts total)
3. Your PICKING ORDER within your tier is determined by 3 weighted criteria:
   - 40% — Weighted Average from last 12 months
   - 40% — Previous RSD Event Campaign Sales (includes Kiosks, Events, Service Events, Service Calls from booth leads, and direct follow-up sales from the event)
   - 20% — 12 months total sales
4. Once your name is in the Shift Schedule it is final until the meeting is over
5. Time Restraint: 2 minutes per turn — no extensions
6. Cannot talk to the MC during shift picking unless it is your turn
7. If you leave the division or don't attend for more than 3 meetings, you start at JV level and work your way up
8. Booth situation will be completed after the meeting — Visualization Manager and Coordinators have final say

### How does Summer Show Picking work?

Fall and Spring: you pick 2 shows during your turn.
Summer: you pick 1 show during your turn.

### If I close an order after the event ends, does it count for my shift average?

No — it does not count for shift average unless it is taken during the event. Once the event is over
it is counted as a FOLLOW UP order. However, follow-up orders still contribute toward 60% of your
show picking because they count as a direct lead from the event.

### What happens if I work a brand new event?

Working a new event will NOT negatively impact your shift average — it will only benefit you. If you
FIND a show the team has not worked before and we end up doing it, you get 1st dibs on working that
show the same year and the following year.

## ATTENDANCE & FINES

### What are the fines for being late to a team meeting?

- Late: $50 Fine
- 1 hour late: Lose first round of picking + $50 Fine
- 2 hours late: Lose 1st and 2nd rounds of picking + $50 Fine
- 3 hours late: Considered missing the meeting — cannot pick any shifts this period + $50 Fine
- Exemptions are for EMERGENCIES ONLY (up to Coordinators and DVM to classify)

### What happens if I no-show a team meeting?

You will be considered no longer part of the events team and you forfeit all shifts you picked.
Team meetings cannot be made up.

### What are the fines for being late to a shift (booth)?

- First Offense: $50
- Second Offense: $100
- Third Offense: $150
- Fourth Offense: $300 + Disciplinary Action from DVM and Coordinator

If a representative knows someone is late but does not report it, and the coordinator finds out,
both parties will be charged a late fine. Late fines must be reported via the Google Form on the team website.

### Can I leave early from a show?

No. Exceptions:
1. Consent from another representative working the event with you
2. Emergency — must contact coordinators immediately

Fines for leaving early are the same as for being late.

### What are the consequences for a No Call No Show at a booth shift?

A No Call No Show (where you don't show up and get no one to cover) results in:
- Charged the full amount for the event
- Must call promoter, explain situation, and apologize (Elite/Varsity = also send a Cutco knife to promoter; JV/Freshmen = optional knife)
- First Offense: $200 Fine + Lose First Round of Picking
- Second Offense: $300 Fine + Lose First 2 Rounds of Picking
- Third Offense: Removal/suspension from events team
- Offenses reset every 2 years

### What if I simply cannot work an event and attempted to get it covered?

If you cannot work AND you tried to reach out to all reps and coordinators:
- You are still charged the full amount for the event
- Must call promoter, explain, and apologize (knife to promoter for Elite/Varsity events)

### What happens if I no-show a team meeting without notice?

You are considered no longer part of the events team and forfeit all shifts you picked.

### What are the stat submission rules?

All stats must be submitted by Tuesday night the following week after the event.
- Stat reminders are sent out Monday and must be completed the following day via Google Form
- Late fine: $50/week

### What are the stats submission links?

## BOOTH RULES & STANDARDS

### When do I need to arrive at a show?

First day of event:
- 1.5 hours early if booth is not set up
- 45 minutes early if personal display is not set up

Following days of events (when booth and knives are already there):
- 15 minutes early

### What do I wear to events?

You are representing a high-class product — dress professionally. Do NOT wear:
- Graphic T-shirts
- Swim trunks / Bathing Suits
- Tank-Tops
- Ripped Jeans / Shorts

### What do I bring to an event?

Check the Booth Storage Info page on risingsunevents.net (Mesa Marketplace tab) for the full
current list for your tier.

### How do I find out what time my event opens/closes?

Google the event. This information is your responsibility to look up.

### What is the Turn System at the booth?

The Turn System is: Cut — Quote — Card — Close

- Cut = gave a food, leather, or penny cut demonstration
- Quote = quoted a price to the customer
- Card = gave a business card OR took someone's information and/or signed them up for sharpening
- Close = closed an order

Rules:
- A turn does NOT count if the customer has a representative on ROR that they work with
- A turn does NOT count if it's a Be-Back
- Representatives working together can create their own turn system if they agree; if not, default to the system above
- Any order discrepancies involving turns should be brought to the coordinator — coordinators have the final say

### Can I work more than 2 parties at once at the booth?

Representatives and assistants/field trainees working together cannot work more than 2 parties at
once UNLESS they are all together as a group. Whoever's turn it is decides which party to work with
and gives the other party to the other rep.

### What are the booth cleanup rules?

All representatives must clean up the booth before the next shift. A booth is considered clean when:
- Trash is taken out
- Cutco is hidden/put away
- Cutting boards are washed
- Food or strips of leather on the ground and table are cleaned up

Fines for not cleaning up:
- First Offense: $50
- Second Offense: $100
- Third Offense: $150
- Fourth Offense: $150 + Disciplinary Action from DVM and Coordinator

These fines apply to ANY rep working the day before, to avoid arguments between reps working
consecutive days. All reps must be communicative with the following day's rep.

### What are the rules for returning a borrowed booth?

- Community booths or leader-owned booths must be returned within 24 hours of the show ending, in the same condition as lent
- All reps' booths must be dropped off within 48 hours of event ending at rep's home or storage unit
- Take inventory of all booth contents BEFORE and AFTER the event
- If any item is damaged or missing, report to the Visualization Coordinator BEFORE the event — costs charged to previous booth renter
- Missing Cutco items: replaced at half retail value
- Other missing/damaged items (banners, tables, tents, etc.): replaced at full retail value

Fines that could apply to booth returns:
- $50 if check-in and check-out form is not filled out within 24 hours of start/end of show
- $25 if tablecloths are not folded properly
- $25 if tablecloths are returned dirty
- $25 if tent walls are not used for overnight shows
- $100 if booth is not dropped off or dropped off in the wrong location
- $50 if storage unit is left unlocked

### Can I sharpen knives at events?

- You can sharpen at Mesa Marketplace, Kiosks, and any traditional event
- Any Key or Elite Events must get coordinator approval for sharpening at a non-designated sharpening event
- Representatives MUST NOT set the precedent that any customer can bring in knives — it is BY APPOINTMENT ONLY with their current representative

### What happens if a show gets canceled?

By taking on a show you assumed the risk. If the same show is rescheduled within 2 campaigns, you
get that event.

## ROR (REP OF RECORD) POLICY

### What is the ROR program?

ROR stands for Rep of Record. The ROR program tracks which Cutco customers belong to which rep.
All active events team members are REQUIRED to be on the ROR Program. All orders and service call
leads taken at the booth must be checked against the most recent ROR List.

The objective is to encourage reps to advertise aggressively to their customers and drive traffic
to the booth, while protecting the relationships reps have built with their customer base.

### What does "active and in good standing" mean for ROR?

To be considered active and in good standing, a rep must be:
- Working a minimum of 4 events/year (not shifts)
- Elite and Varsity must be actively marketing monthly to customers (texting or emailing)
- JV must have a CRM
- Freshmen must complete Events Training/Field Training requirements, work their first shift, and work a minimum of 1 event per campaign
- Attending 2/3 meetings (either virtual or Zoom) between the last 3 meetings
- Not subject to any disciplinary action

### How do I check the ROR list?

1. Download the Master ROR Spreadsheet onto your phone or device (from VectorConnect)
2. Pull it up as soon as a customer mentions they are a past customer and may have a rep
3. Type the customer's last name into the Search bar or use Command F
4. Can also search by address, first name, husband's name, or phone number

### What are the ROR tip-out fees?

When a customer on the ROR list places an order through a different rep, the original rep must pay
a ROR tip-out fee to the rep who closed the sale:
- Under 1000 CPO = 10%
- 1000–1999 CPO = 15%
- 2000+ CPO = 20%
- Service Call or Service Event = 25%

Payment must be made within 7–10 days between the individual reps. If there is a failure to pay the
ROR fee, notify the coordinator. Failure to pay subjects people to disciplinary action, including
removal from the ROR program and possible suspension from the team.

### What counts as a referral order for ROR?

If a customer comes to the booth and their referral/friend/family member purchases at the booth, and
the customer owns Cutco, the representative must check the ROR for that customer.

### What do I do if a customer does NOT want to work with their current representative?

- Hold the order and bring it to the coordinator's attention
- There must be a genuine complaint/issue — just because a client does not KNOW the representative does NOT validate a transfer
- If you have documentation or a letter, provide it to the coordinator(s)
- We encourage reps to work it out amongst themselves first; if uncomfortable, bring it to the coordinator
- Just because a customer wants to switch does NOT mean you keep the full order without any tip-out — all transfers and tip-out percentages are handled on a case-by-case basis

### What are the fines for unauthorized order transfers?

- First Offense: Order gets flipped with no tip-out + conversation with DVM
- Second Offense: Suspension for the rest of the campaign
- Third Offense: Suspension from the team

### What about CTR in ROR calculations?

Double the CTR and remove that from the CPO when calculating tip-out.

### What if the customer says a rep's name but they don't pop up on the ROR list?

The order gets transferred based on NAME DROP.

## SERVICE EVENTS

### What is a Team Service Event?

A Team Service Event is a service event with more than 2 people present. All RSD Team Service Event
Standards apply to any service event with more than 2 people.

### What are the attendance rules for service events?

- All representatives must show up to their scheduled service event
- If you no-show: charged the full cost of the service event
- No call no show treated as a serious violation:
  - First Offense: $200 Fine + Lose First Round of Picking
  - Second Offense: $300 Fine + Lose First 2 Rounds of Picking
  - Third Offense: Removal/suspension from events team
  - Offenses reset every year
- If you signed up and no longer plan to attend: notify Service Event Coordinator minimum 2 weeks in advance to avoid being charged

### What display products must I bring to a Service Event?

Required display products:
- Ultimate Set
- Flatware Set
- Accessory Package
- Aspiring Cookware Set (can split with another representative)
- Elite Members only: Biz Gifts

Service Event Supplies are provided by the team and are NOT required. There is a $50 fine for not
having your required display pillars.

### What are the timing rules for service events?

- All reps must be fully set up 15 minutes before scheduled time
- Cannot begin to tear down until 6:00 PM (unless there are NO appointments left between any rep)
- Fines for late or early leave:
  - First Offense: $50
  - Second Offense: $100
  - Third Offense: $200
  - Fourth Offense: $300 + Disciplinary Action from DVM and Coordinator

### What are appointment slot requirements at service events?

- All reps are required to have a minimum of 45-minute appointment slots
- 30 minutes is allowed if you have a sharpening assistant with you

### What is the factory sharpening fee?

Factory sharpening is $30 per client sending in products to the factory. You can choose to waive
this fee or offer to pay shipping, but if you charge the client you cannot charge anything more or less than $30.

### What do I do if there is no greeter for a service event?

Whoever is available should greet the client and inspect the knives if their representative is busy,
and inform the rep subtly that their client has arrived.

### When must I submit stats and calendar for service events?

- Submit a screenshot of your calendar to the Service Event Coordinator the day before the event
- Orders must be tabbed under the correct event and stats submitted by the following Tuesday to avoid a $50 late fine
